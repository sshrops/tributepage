A brief summary of Oda-san and his most legendary title.

Eiichiro Oda was born on January 1, 1975 in Kumamato City, Japan. At a young age Oda-san was inspired to become a manga artist by his love of manga and tv as well as a desire to avoid a boring everyday job. His biggest influences of his early years revolved around the popular Dragon Ball Franchise as well as his favorite show Vicky the Viking. When he eventually turned 17, Oda-san submitted an original work titled Wanted! To Weekly Shonen Jump magazine. This title won several awards and eventually landed him a job at the same publication. A few years later in 1997, Oda-san released One Piece. This new series would go on to become the most popular manga of all time. Since its debut Oda-san has won countless awards and praise for his manga and its anime adaptation. Later in 2015 the Gummies Book of World Records awarded One piece the record for most copies published of the same comic book series. One piece is currently still ongoing as of 2018 and shows no signs of slowing down in the near future. It has eclipsed its humble beginnings as just a manga and has become a phenomenon in japan and among many fans located all over the world.
